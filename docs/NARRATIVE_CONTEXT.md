## The Four Rules (Recap)

Every market, every technology transition, every empire — they all follow the same four dynamics. There has never been an exception.

### 1. Efficiency of Intelligence
The universe rewards whatever does more with less. Every surviving system is better at turning energy into information than the system it replaced.

**In markets:** This is why compute stocks compound faster than the rest of the economy. GPUs turn electricity into intelligence more efficiently than CPUs did. Photonics turns electricity into bandwidth more efficiently than copper did. The rate of efficiency gain *is* the return.

**What to look for:** Technologies that improve energy-to-information conversion by multiple orders of magnitude. Silicon photonics. Nuclear for data centers. Neuromorphic chips.

### 2. Compression
Intelligence is representing a vast reality in a much smaller form without losing what matters. Brains do it. Theories do it. Prices do it. They are the same operation.

**In markets:** A P/E ratio compresses a company's entire future into a single number. Our BM25 memory compresses past trades into retrievable patterns. Valuation models that compress the *wrong* data (trailing DCF on narrative-driven stocks) produce garbage.

**What to look for:** When a stock's price compresses a narrative that hasn't materialized in earnings yet (like photonics at 300x P/E), the compression ratio tells you how much future is already priced in. High compression = late stage. Low compression = early stage.

### 3. Coherence
Complex systems hold together because their parts synchronize faster than the noise around them. When synchronization fails, what looks like collapse is desynchronization made visible.

**In markets:** The AI value chain is one system trying to stay coherent: GPUs compute → copper carries → photonics accelerates → power grid feeds → cooling dissipates. Every break in this chain forces re-synchronization. *Tradeable opportunities live in the gaps between breaks.*

**What to look for:** Correlation breakage. When photonics stocks rally 400% but power stocks are flat, the system is desynchronized. The gap will close. The question is which side moves.

### 4. Selection
Patterns that copy themselves faster than their rivals dominate the medium they live in. Genes do this in biology. Ideas do it in culture. Memecoins do it in markets. Truth is not part of the selection criteria. Replication is.

**In markets:** A narrative that spreads faster than competing narratives dominates capital allocation. Lumentum's EML lasers have no viable second source through 2027 — that's selection in action. The stock replicates faster because the underlying technology has no competitor.

**What to look for:** Narrative velocity — how fast a thesis is spreading across earnings calls, analyst notes, and news. The stock with the highest replication rate wins, regardless of whether it's the "best" technology.

## How This Maps to Asset Classes

### Photonics / Optical Interconnects

**Narrative phase:** Late discovery → priced in (mid-2026)

Aschenbrenner doesn't mention photonics explicitly, but his compute scaling framework implies it: at 1M+ GPU clusters, copper interconnects hit physical bandwidth and power limits. Light *must* replace electricity for data movement. Nvidia's $4B bet on Coherent and Lumentum confirms this.

**Coherence check:** Photonics stocks up 200-400% in 6 months while the underlying earnings haven't caught up. This is a compressional gap — the narrative has been compressed into price faster than the fundamentals can verify. It's not wrong, but it's early in the adoption curve and late in the discovery curve.

**Selection check:** Lumentum wins because EML lasers have no second source through 2027. The narrative replicates fastest because there's no competing story.

### Power Generation (Nuclear, Gas, Fuel Cells)

**Narrative phase:** Pre-discovery (mid-2026)

**This is the largest coherence gap in markets right now.** Power stocks (CEG, VST, TLN) are down 10-19% in 3 months while AI infrastructure spending is accelerating. The desynchronization between compute buildout and power infrastructure is the trade.

Aschenbrenner's numbers make this unavoidable:
- AI going from 1-2% of US electricity → ~20% by 2030
- Utilities revised 5-year growth forecast from 2.6% → 4.7% (still undershooting)
- Natural gas can scale: 40 rigs, $100B capex, 2-year build cycle for 100 GW
- Nuclear has the baseload but the timeline

**The four rules:** Power generation is the coherence bottleneck of the entire AI system. When the system desynchronizes (compute grows faster than power), the side that needs to catch up (power) becomes the trade.

**Key tickers:** CEG (nuclear), VST (gas + renewables), TLN (energy transition), BE (fuel cells), EQT (natural gas), URNM (uranium ETF)

### Rare Earths / Critical Minerals

**Narrative phase:** Very early pre-discovery

The US $12B critical minerals reserve, supply chain reshoring, and the sheer volume of GPUs, magnets, and power infrastructure needed creates a structural demand story that markets haven't absorbed.

**Key tickers:** MP (US rare earth leader), UUUU (uranium + rare earths)

### Bitcoin Miners as AI Data Centers

Situational Awareness LP holds 8+ bitcoin miners (CORZ, IREN, APLD, CIFR, RIOT, HUT, BTDR, CLSK, BITF). The thesis: these companies already own power capacity, cooling infrastructure, and grid interconnection — exactly what AI data centers need. The market still sees them as crypto plays.

### Storage / Memory

## Narrative Lifecycle Phases

Use this to judge where any thesis is in its lifecycle:

| Phase | Characteristics | What to Do | Market Behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-discovery** | Nobody talks about it. No analyst coverage. Price flat or declining despite structural tailwinds. | Accumulate. No one else is looking. | No correlation to the thesis yet. |
| **Discovery** | Early adopters find it. 1-2 analysts publish. Price starts lifting. | Add aggressively. Narrative velocity accelerating. | Price leads earnings. |
| **Priced in** | Everyone owns it. Massively rerated. P/E at extremes. Conference mentions. | Trim. Take profits. The easy money is made. | Earnings catch up slowly. Multiple compression risk. |
| **Exhaustion** | Forward guidance disappoints. Narrative fatigue. Rotation begins. | Exit. The next pre-discovery phase has started elsewhere. | Price leads earnings down. |

## How to Use This Context

Every time you run analysis, ask:

1. **What phase is this narrative in?** (Pre-discovery → Discovery → Priced in → Exhaustion)
2. **Is there a coherence gap?** (What part of the AI value chain is desynchronized?)
3. **What is the compression ratio?** (How much future is already in the price?)
4. **Who wins selection?** (Which ticker has the narrative replicating fastest?)

A "BUY" signal from the technical system on a stock in the **priced-in** phase is less reliable than a "HOLD" on a stock in **pre-discovery**. Narrative phase overrides technical signal.

**The most important question right now:** Power infrastructure is the only part of the AI value chain that hasn't rerated. Is the coherence gap closing, or is the market right to ignore it?
